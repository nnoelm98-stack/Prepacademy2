Linear Equations — Two Variables
Master the slope-intercept and standard forms of linear equations, learn what slope and y-intercept mean in context, and practice reading and writing linear equations from graphs and tables.
14 min read3 practice questions+20 pts on completion
The two key forms of a linear equation
A linear equation in two variables describes a straight line — every point (x, y) that satisfies the equation lies on that line. The SAT uses two main forms.
Slope-intercept form
y = mx + b
m = slope (rate of change) · b = y-intercept (value of y when x = 0). Best for reading slope and intercept directly, graphing, and word problems involving rates.
Standard form
Ax + By = C
A, B, C are integers. Best for systems of equations and finding intercepts: set x = 0 for y-intercept, set y = 0 for x-intercept.
slope m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁) = rise / run
Positive slope → line rises left to right · Negative slope → line falls left to right · Zero slope → horizontal line · Undefined slope → vertical line
Worked example — writing an equation from two points
Find the equation of the line through (2, 5) and (6, 13).

Step 1: slope = (13 − 5) / (6 − 2) = 8 / 4 = 2
Step 2: use y = mx + b → 5 = 2(2) + b → b = 1
Equation: y = 2x + 1

Check with (6, 13): y = 2(6) + 1 = 13 ✓
What slope and intercept mean in real-world contexts
The SAT frequently asks you to interpret the slope and y-intercept of a linear model — not just calculate them. The key is connecting the math to the story.
Slope = rate of change
The slope tells you how much y changes for each one-unit increase in x. In a word problem, ask: what are the units of the slope?
y = 0.15x + 20 (cost of phone plan)
Slope 0.15 = $0.15 per minute of usage
Each extra minute costs $0.15 more.
y-intercept = starting value
The y-intercept is the value of y when x = 0 — the baseline, initial amount, or fixed cost before any change occurs.
y = 0.15x + 20 (cost of phone plan)
y-intercept 20 = $20 flat monthly fee
The cost before any minutes are used.
SAT strategy tips
Convert to slope-intercept for reading
If an equation is given in standard form (Ax + By = C), solve for y to get slope-intercept form. This makes slope and y-intercept immediately visible without extra calculation.
Use two points to find slope first
When given a table or graph, always find slope first using two clear points. Then substitute one point into y = mx + b to find b. Never try to find b without finding m first.
Parallel lines have equal slopes
Two lines are parallel if and only if their slopes are equal (and they have different y-intercepts). Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals of each other: m₁ × m₂ = −1.
x-intercept: set y = 0
The x-intercept is where the line crosses the x-axis — set y = 0 and solve for x. The y-intercept is where it crosses the y-axis — set x = 0 and solve for y.
Practice questions
Problem
A line passes through the points (−1, 3) and (3, 11).
Find the slope first using the two points, then substitute one point into y = mx + b to find the y-intercept.
Question 1 · Slope-Intercept Form
Which equation correctly describes the line through (−1, 3) and (3, 11)?
Ay = 2x + 5
By = 3x + 6
Cy = 2x − 5
Dy = 4x + 7
Correct! y = 2x + 5
m = (11 − 3) / (3 − (−1)) = 8 / 4 = 2
Using (−1, 3): 3 = 2(−1) + b → b = 5
y = 2x + 5
Check (3, 11): 2(3) + 5 = 11 ✓
Not quite. Start by finding slope: m = (y₂ − y₁)/(x₂ − x₁) = (11 − 3)/(3 − (−1)). Then substitute one point into y = mx + b to solve for b.
m = 8/4 = 2 · Then: 3 = 2(−1) + b → b = 5
Answer: y = 2x + 5
Problem
A water tank initially holds 400 gallons. Water drains out at a constant rate of 25 gallons per hour.
V = 400 − 25t
where V = volume (gallons) and t = time (hours). Answer the question about this linear model.
Question 2 · Interpreting Linear Models
In the equation V = 400 − 25t, what does the value 400 represent?
AThe rate at which water drains per hour
BThe number of hours until the tank is empty
CThe initial volume of water in the tank when t = 0
DThe total volume of water drained after 400 hours
Correct! In V = 400 − 25t, this is slope-intercept form with the y-intercept equal to 400. The y-intercept represents the value of V when t = 0 — the starting volume before any time has passed. When t = 0: V = 400 − 25(0) = 400 gallons. The slope −25 represents the drain rate (gallons per hour), not 400.
Not quite. Write this as V = −25t + 400 — now it clearly matches y = mx + b. What does the b term (the constant, not multiplied by t) represent in a linear model? It's the value of the output when the input (t) equals zero.
Problem
Line ℓ has equation 4x − 2y = 10. Line k is parallel to line ℓ and passes through the point (0, 3).
Step 1: Convert line ℓ to slope-intercept form to find its slope. Step 2: Use the same slope for line k. Step 3: Use the given point to write line k's equation.
Question 3 · Parallel Lines
What is the equation of line k?
Ay = −2x + 3
By = 2x − 5
Cy = 2x + 3
Dy = 4x + 3
Correct! y = 2x + 3
Line ℓ: 4x − 2y = 10
−2y = −4x + 10 → y = 2x − 5
Slope of ℓ = 2
Line k has same slope (parallel): m = 2
Passes through (0, 3) → b = 3
Line k: y = 2x + 3 ✓
Not quite. First, solve 4x − 2y = 10 for y to find the slope of line ℓ. Parallel lines share the same slope. Then, since line k passes through (0, 3), that point is the y-intercept (x = 0), so b = 3.
4x − 2y = 10 → y = 2x − 5 · slope = 2
Line k: y = 2x + 3
