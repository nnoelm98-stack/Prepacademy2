PrepAcademy · Lesson 1.1 · Central Ideas & Details
Adapted from a scientific article on bioluminescence
Bioluminescence — the ability of living organisms to produce light through chemical reactions — is far more common in the natural world than most people realize. While fireflies are the most familiar example, the majority of bioluminescent organisms live in the deep ocean, where sunlight never penetrates. Scientists studying bioluminescence have found that despite its widespread occurrence, the ability evolved independently dozens of times — suggesting that light production confers significant survival advantages across diverse environments.
Central Idea
